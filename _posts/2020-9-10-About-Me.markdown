# You can find my CV [here](https://fmjohnson97.github.io/assets/files/FaithJohnsonCV.pdf) and my résumé [here](https://fmjohnson97.github.io/assets/files/Faith_Johnson_Official_Resume.pdf)

I am a second year Ph.D. student at Rutgers Univeristy in the ECE department, where I focus on the intersection of computer vision, machine learning, and robotics. My advisor is [Professor Kristin Dana](https://www.ece.rutgers.edu/~kdana/). Before that, I got by bachelor's degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering also from Rutgers University. 


# Research
My next research focus deals with human-robot interaction and robot navigation with humans in the loop. Stay tuned for more!

My previous work deals with autonomous vehicle steering angle prediction. We created a network, [Feudal Steering](https://fmjohnson97.github.io/feudal-steering/), that uses hierarchical networks to exploit the temporal abstraction inherent in driving tasks to make predicting steering angles from dash-cam images easier. 

# Other Projects
I have had the opportunity to work on several different projects with other teams.

At [SRI International](https://www.sri.com/computer-vision/) in 2020, I created baseline agents for the [DARPA Machine Common Sense](https://www.darpa.mil/program/machine-common-sense) project.
